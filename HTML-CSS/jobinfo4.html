Paleontologists

Description

A paleontologist is a scientist who studies fossils and contributes to research on their historical, sociological and archeological impacts. These individuals often specialize in a specific era or type of fossil, which may influence some of their typical tasks.

Average Salary

In Halifax, NS, the average salary for a paleontologist is around $73,146 per year or $35 per hour. Entry-level positions start at around $39,500 annually, while top earners can make up to $129,000.

Employers

  • Universities
  • Museums
  • Research institutions
  • Government agencies
  • Consulting firms
  • Environmental studies
  • Geological studies

Related Careers

  • museum curator: manages and cares for a museums's collections, including acquiring, preserving, researching, and displaying artifacts and cultural heritage items, while also engaging the public through exhibitions and education
  • science writer: writes articles for general circulation magaines, science magazines geared to the general public, magazines for scientists and engineers, and newspapers.
  • park ranger: Protects and preserve parklands and protected areas, with duties including law enforcement, wildlife and land management, community engagement, recreation area maintenance, and firefighting.
  • archaeologist: investigate past human cultures and societies by excavating and analyzing artifacts, sites, and landscapes to understand human history, technology, social structures, and cultural practices.
